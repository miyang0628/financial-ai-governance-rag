### Overview — Korean AI Basic Act

**Legal status:** Mandatory (강제적 기본법) — administrative fines up to KRW 30,000,000 (approx. EUR 20,000) under Article 43.

**Purpose (Article 1):** This Act aims to protect the rights and dignity of citizens and to contribute to improving quality of life and strengthening national competitiveness by stipulating basic matters necessary for the sound development of artificial intelligence and the establishment of a trust foundation.

**Effective date:** January 22, 2026 (with partial provisions effective July 21, 2026).

**Regulatory scope:** Applies extraterritorially — actions conducted outside Korea are subject to this Act if they affect the domestic market or users (Article 4(1)).

**Supervisory authority:** Ministry of Science and ICT (과학기술정보통신부); National AI Strategy Committee (국가인공지능전략위원회) chaired by the President.

**Key financial AI provisions:** Article 2(4)(사) explicitly lists credit screening (대출 심사) as a High-Impact AI domain. This is the primary legal basis for classifying financial lending AI as High-Impact AI under Korean law.

**Comparison with EU AI Act:** Both are mandatory with penalties. Korean Act uses "High-Impact AI (고영향 인공지능)" corresponding to EU AI Act "High-Risk AI." Korean penalties (KRW 30M) are substantially lower than EU penalties (up to EUR 35M or 7% of worldwide turnover).

### Article 1 — Purpose

This Act aims to protect the rights and dignity of citizens and to contribute to improving quality of life and strengthening national competitiveness by stipulating the basic matters necessary for the sound development of artificial intelligence and the establishment of a trust foundation.

---

### Article 2 — Definitions

1. **"Artificial intelligence (AI)"** means the electronic implementation of intellectual capabilities possessed by humans, such as learning, reasoning, perception, judgment, and language understanding.

2. **"AI system"** means an AI-based system that infers outputs such as predictions, recommendations, and decisions that affect real and virtual environments for given objectives with varying levels of autonomy and adaptability.

3. **"AI technology"** means hardware and software technology necessary to implement artificial intelligence, or the technology for its utilization.

4. **"High-Impact AI (고영향 인공지능)"** means an AI system that is likely to have significant impact on or pose risks to human life, physical safety, and fundamental rights, and that is utilized in any of the following domains:
   - (가) Energy supply under Article 2(1) of the Energy Act
   - (나) Drinking water production processes under Article 3(1) of the Drinking Water Management Act
   - (다) Provision and utilization system of healthcare under Article 3(1) of the Framework Act on Health and Medical Services
   - (라) Development and use of medical devices under Article 2(1) of the Medical Devices Act and digital medical devices under Article 2(2) of the Digital Medical Products Act
   - (마) Safe management and operation of nuclear materials and nuclear facilities under the Act on Physical Protection of and Radiological Emergency Preparedness for Nuclear Facilities
   - (바) Analysis and use of biometric information (personal information relating to physical, physiological, and behavioral characteristics of an individual, such as face, fingerprint, iris, and palm vein, capable of identifying an individual) for criminal investigation or arrest duties
   - **(사) Judgment or evaluation that significantly affects the rights and obligations of individuals, such as recruitment and credit (loan) screening — 채용, 대출 심사 등 개인의 권리·의무 관계에 중대한 영향을 미치는 판단 또는 평가**
   - (아) Major operation and management of transportation means, transportation facilities, and transportation systems under Article 2(1) through (3) of the Traffic Safety Act
   - (자) Decision-making by national agencies, local governments, and public institutions affecting citizens, such as qualification verification and decisions necessary for providing public services, or fee collection
   - (차) Student assessment in early childhood education, elementary education, and secondary education under Article 9(1) of the Framework Act on Education
   - **(카) Other domains with significant impact on the protection of human life, physical safety, and fundamental rights, as prescribed by Presidential Decree**

5. **"Generative AI (생성형 인공지능)"** means an AI system that generates outputs such as text, sound, images, video, and other various results by mimicking the structure and characteristics of input data.

6. **"AI industry"** means the industry that develops, manufactures, produces, or distributes products using AI or AI technology (hereinafter "AI products"), or provides services related thereto (hereinafter "AI services").

7. **"AI business operator (인공지능사업자)"** means a corporation, organization, individual, or national agency engaged in business related to the AI industry, corresponding to any of the following:
   - (가) AI developer-operator: a person who develops and provides AI
   - (나) AI user-operator: a person who provides AI products or AI services using AI provided by an AI developer-operator

8. **"User"** means a person who receives AI products or AI services.

9. **"Affected person"** means a person whose life, physical safety, and fundamental rights are significantly affected by AI products or AI services.

**Financial AI classification note (G4):** Article 2(4)(사) — "judgment or evaluation that significantly affects individual rights and obligations, such as recruitment and credit (loan) screening" — is the explicit basis for classifying credit scoring and loan assessment AI as High-Impact AI under Korean law. This provision parallels EU AI Act Annex III §5(b) (creditworthiness evaluation as High-Risk AI). The clause (카) allows Presidential Decree to expand the list, potentially adding insurance pricing AI that is not yet explicitly listed.

### Article 3 — Basic Principles and Responsibilities of the State

1. AI technology and the AI industry shall develop in a direction that improves the quality of life of citizens by enhancing safety and trustworthiness.

2. Affected persons shall be provided with clear and meaningful explanations, to the extent technically and reasonably feasible, regarding the main criteria and principles used in deriving the final output of AI.

3. The State and local governments shall respect the creative spirit of AI business operators and shall endeavor to create a safe AI use environment.

4. The State and local governments shall devise policies to enable all citizens to stably adapt to changes in all areas including social, economic, and cultural domains and daily life brought by AI.

5. **(Effective July 21, 2026)** The State and local governments shall ensure the participation of vulnerable groups including persons with disabilities and the elderly (hereinafter "AI vulnerable groups") who have difficulty using AI products or AI services in the process of developing and establishing AI-related policies, and shall endeavor to ensure that their opinions are reflected.

**Financial AI note (G3):** Article 3(2) is the primary Korean legal basis for the right to explanation — affected persons are entitled to meaningful explanations of the main criteria used in AI output. This is the Korean equivalent of EU AI Act Article 86 (Right to Explanation of Individual Decision-Making). For credit rejection decisions, financial institutions must be able to explain the main factors driving the AI decision. Article 3(5) mandates protection of vulnerable groups in AI policy — relevant for ensuring AI credit and insurance products do not systematically disadvantage elderly or disabled customers.

---

### Article 4 — Scope of Application

1. This Act applies even to actions conducted outside of Korea if they affect the domestic market or users.

2. This Act does not apply to AI developed and used solely for national defense or national security purposes, as prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Financial AI note (G4):** Article 4(1) is the Korean AI Basic Act's extra-territorial application clause — equivalent to EU AI Act Article 2(1)(c). A foreign-developed AI system whose outputs affect Korean loan applicants or insurance policyholders is subject to this Act. Financial institutions must apply Korean AI Basic Act governance requirements to all AI systems affecting Korean customers, regardless of where those systems are developed.

### Article 15 — Policies on AI Training Data

1. The Minister of Science and ICT shall promote necessary policies in consultation with the heads of relevant central administrative agencies for the production, collection, management, distribution, utilization promotion, and quality assurance of training data.

2. The government may select support target projects and provide support within the budget for the efficient promotion of policies related to the production, collection, management, distribution, and utilization of training data.

**Financial AI note (G1):** Article 15 establishes the government framework for AI training data quality — the Korean basis for data governance requirements that parallel EU AI Act Article 10. Financial institutions must ensure training data quality, representativeness, and appropriate governance as part of High-Impact AI compliance.

### Article 28 — Establishment of Private Autonomous AI Ethics Committees

1. The following institutions or organizations may establish a Private Autonomous AI Ethics Committee (민간자율인공지능윤리위원회):
   - (1) Educational institutions and research institutions to which persons conducting AI technology research and development belong
   - (2) AI business operators
   - (3) Other AI technology-related institutions prescribed by Presidential Decree

2. The Private Autonomous AI Ethics Committee shall autonomously perform the following duties:
   - (1) Verification of compliance with AI ethical principles in AI technology research, development, and utilization
   - (2) Investigation and research on safety and human rights violations in AI technology research, development, and utilization
   - (3) Investigation and supervision of procedures and results of AI technology research, development, and utilization
   - (4) Education on ethical principles for researchers and employees of the relevant institution or organization
   - (5) Preparation of sector-specific AI ethics guidelines appropriate for AI technology research, development, and utilization
   - (6) Other duties necessary for implementing ethical principles

**Financial AI note (G4):** Article 28 provides the statutory basis for financial institution AI Review Committees (AI 심의위원회) — the key institutional governance mechanism examined in this research. Financial institutions may establish such committees as Private Autonomous AI Ethics Committees. The committee's mandate covers the full scope of AI ethical governance required under the Act.

### Article 29 — Measures for Establishing AI Trust Foundation

The government shall prepare the following measures to minimize potential risks of AI on citizens' lives and to establish a trust foundation for safe AI use:

1. Creating a safe and trustworthy AI use environment.
2. Forecasting and predicting the impact of AI use on citizens' daily lives and reforming related laws and institutions.
3. Supporting the development and dissemination of safety technology and certification technology to ensure AI safety and trustworthiness.
4. Education and publicity for realizing a safe and trustworthy AI society and practicing AI ethics.
5. Supporting AI business operators' autonomous enactment and implementation of safety and trustworthiness-related codes.
6. Supporting and spreading private activities such as autonomous cooperation and enactment of ethical guidelines by organizations composed of AI business operators, users, etc. for promoting AI safety and trustworthiness.

### Article 30 — Support for AI Safety and Trustworthiness Verification and Certification

1. The Minister of Science and ICT may promote the following projects to support verification and certification activities (hereinafter "verification and certification") that organizations voluntarily promote to ensure AI safety and trustworthiness:
   - (1) Dissemination of guidelines on AI development
   - (2) Support for research on verification and certification
   - (3) Support for construction and operation of equipment and systems used for verification and certification
   - (4) Support for training of specialists needed for verification and certification
   - (5) Other matters prescribed by Presidential Decree to support verification and certification

3. When an AI business operator provides High-Impact AI, the operator shall endeavor to obtain verification and certification in advance.

4. When national agencies intend to use High-Impact AI, they shall preferentially consider products or services based on AI that has obtained verification and certification.

**Financial AI note (G4):** Article 30(3) establishes a "best effort" obligation for High-Impact AI providers to obtain voluntary certification before providing the system. While not mandatory (unlike EU AI Act Art.43 conformity assessment), this provision incentivizes pre-deployment certification — relevant for financial institutions offering credit scoring AI to other banks or deploying insurance pricing AI products.

### Article 31 — Obligation to Ensure AI Transparency

1. When an AI business operator intends to provide products or services using High-Impact AI or generative AI, the operator shall **notify users in advance** of the fact that the product or service is operated based on such AI.

2. When an AI business operator provides generative AI or products or services using it, the operator shall **indicate that the output was generated by generative AI**.

3. When an AI business operator provides outputs such as virtual sounds, images, or videos that are difficult to distinguish from reality using an AI system, the operator shall notify or indicate in a manner that allows users to clearly recognize that such output was generated by an AI system. In cases where the output constitutes or forms part of an artistic or creative expression, the notification or indication may be made in a manner that does not impede the display or enjoyment of the work.

4. Other matters necessary regarding the method of advance notification under paragraph 1, the indication under paragraph 2, the notification or indication under paragraph 3, and exceptions thereto shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Penalty:** Failure to provide advance notification under Article 31(1) is subject to an administrative fine of up to KRW 30,000,000 under Article 43(1)(1).

**Financial AI note (G3):** Article 31 is the primary Korean transparency obligation — the Korean equivalent of EU AI Act Article 50. Key requirements for financial institutions: (1) Customers must be notified in advance that a product or service is operated by High-Impact AI — applicable to AI-assisted credit decisions, insurance pricing, and customer service chatbots; (2) AI-generated content must be labeled; (3) Deepfakes or realistic synthetic content must be disclosed. Simultaneous compliance with Article 31 and EU AI Act Article 50 is achievable through a unified AI disclosure policy.

### Article 32 — Obligation to Ensure AI Safety

1. AI business operators shall fulfill the following matters to ensure the safety of AI systems whose cumulative computation used for training meets or exceeds the standard prescribed by Presidential Decree:
   - (1) Identification, assessment, and mitigation of risks throughout the entire AI lifecycle
   - (2) Establishment of a risk management system to monitor and respond to AI-related safety incidents

2. AI business operators shall submit the results of implementation of the matters under paragraph 1 to the Minister of Science and ICT.

3. The Minister of Science and ICT shall determine and publicly announce specific implementation methods for the matters under paragraph 1 subparagraphs and submission of results under paragraph 2.

**Financial AI note (G2/G4):** Article 32 establishes the Korean equivalent of the EU AI Act's risk management system requirement (Art.9). The obligation applies to AI systems above a computation threshold (to be specified by Presidential Decree — likely targeting large-scale AI models). Key requirements: (1) Lifecycle risk identification, assessment, and mitigation — parallel to NIST MAP·MEASURE·MANAGE functions; (2) Risk management system establishment including safety incident monitoring — parallel to NIST MANAGE 4.3 and EU AI Act Art.73 serious incident reporting. Financial institutions operating large-scale credit or insurance AI models must implement this dual system.

### Article 33 — Confirmation of High-Impact AI

1. When an AI business operator provides AI or products or services using it, the operator shall **review in advance** whether the AI constitutes High-Impact AI, and if necessary, may request the Minister of Science and ICT to confirm whether it qualifies as High-Impact AI.

2. When a request under paragraph 1 is received, the Minister of Science and ICT shall confirm whether it qualifies as High-Impact AI, and may establish a specialized committee to receive related advice if necessary.

3. The Minister of Science and ICT may establish and disseminate guidelines on the criteria and examples of High-Impact AI.

4. Other matters necessary regarding the confirmation procedure under paragraph 1 shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Financial AI note (G4):** Article 33 establishes the pre-deployment High-Impact AI screening obligation — the Korean equivalent of EU AI Act Article 6 classification rules. Before deploying any AI system, financial institutions must self-assess whether it qualifies as High-Impact AI under Article 2(4). Credit screening AI under clause (사) is definitively High-Impact. The Ministry of Science and ICT can issue guidelines clarifying edge cases such as insurance pricing AI or robo-advisory AI. The AI Review Committee should document this screening as a mandatory pre-deployment step.

### Article 34 — Responsibilities of Business Operators Related to High-Impact AI

1. When an AI business operator provides High-Impact AI or products or services using it, the operator shall fulfill the following measures as prescribed by Presidential Decree to ensure the safety and trustworthiness of High-Impact AI:
   - **(1) Establishment and operation of risk management measures (위험관리방안의 수립·운영)**
   - **(2) Establishment and implementation of plans to explain**, to the extent technically feasible, the final output derived by AI, the main criteria used in deriving the final output, and an overview of training data used in developing and utilizing AI
   - **(3) Establishment and operation of user protection measures (이용자 보호 방안의 수립·운영)**
   - **(4) Human oversight and supervision of High-Impact AI (고영향 인공지능에 대한 사람의 관리·감독)**
   - **(5) Preparation and retention of documents confirming the content of measures taken to ensure safety and trustworthiness**
   - (6) Other matters deliberated and resolved by the National AI Strategy Committee to ensure safety and trustworthiness of High-Impact AI

2. The Minister of Science and ICT shall determine and publicly announce specific matters regarding the measures under paragraph 1, and may recommend that AI business operators comply with these.

3. If an AI business operator has fulfilled measures equivalent to those in paragraph 1 pursuant to other laws and regulations as prescribed by Presidential Decree, the operator shall be deemed to have fulfilled the measures under paragraph 1.

**Financial AI note — Article 34 G-axis mapping:**

| Sub-clause | Requirement | G-axis | EU AI Act Parallel |
|---|---|---|---|
| §34①(1) | Risk management measures | G4 | Art.9 Risk Management System |
| §34①(2) | Explanation of AI output and main criteria | G3 | Art.13 + Art.86 |
| §34①(3) | User protection measures | G2 | Art.26(11) + Art.27 |
| §34①(4) | Human oversight and supervision | G2 | Art.14 Human Oversight |
| §34①(5) | Documentation and retention | G3 | Art.11 Technical Documentation |

Article 34(3) provides a financial sector equivalence clause — parallel to EU AI Act Art.9(10) and Art.17(3). If a financial institution has fulfilled equivalent measures under financial services law (e.g., FSS model risk guidelines, internal audit requirements), these are deemed to satisfy Article 34(1) obligations. The AI Review Committee must document which financial regulatory measures satisfy which Article 34(1) requirements.

### Article 35 — High-Impact AI Impact Assessment

1. When an AI business operator provides products or services using High-Impact AI, the operator shall **endeavor to assess in advance** the impact on the fundamental rights of persons (hereinafter "impact assessment"). In conducting the impact assessment, the characteristics of AI vulnerable groups shall be reflected in consideration of the nature of the product or service using High-Impact AI. *(Amendment effective July 21, 2026)*

2. When national agencies intend to use products or services using High-Impact AI, they shall **preferentially consider** products or services for which impact assessments have been conducted.

3. Other matters necessary regarding the specific content and methods of impact assessments shall be prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Financial AI note (G2):** Article 35 establishes the Korean equivalent of EU AI Act Article 27 (Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment). Key differences: Korean Act uses "endeavor to" (노력하여야) — a best-effort obligation rather than a mandatory obligation; EU AI Act Art.27 is mandatory for deployers of Annex III §5(b) and §5(c) systems. For financial institutions, conducting impact assessments is strongly recommended best practice even where not strictly mandatory under Korean law, as it demonstrates comprehensive governance and aligns with the preferential treatment given to impact-assessed AI by national agencies (§35②). Amendment effective July 21, 2026 specifically requires that AI vulnerable groups' characteristics be considered in impact assessments.

### Article 36 — Designation of Domestic Representative

1. An AI business operator without an address or place of business in Korea that meets the criteria prescribed by Presidential Decree (e.g., number of users, revenue) shall designate by document a person to act as representative (hereinafter "domestic representative") for the following matters and report this to the Minister of Science and ICT:
   - (1) Submission of implementation results under Article 32(2)
   - (2) Requests for confirmation of High-Impact AI qualification under Article 33(1)
   - (3) Support for fulfilling safety and trustworthiness measures under Article 34(1) (including inspection of the currency and accuracy of documents under Article 34(1)(5))

2. Domestic representatives shall have an address or place of business in Korea.

3. If a domestic representative violates this Act in connection with matters under paragraph 1, the AI business operator that designated the domestic representative shall be deemed to have committed the act.

**Financial AI note (G4):** Article 36 is the Korean AI Basic Act's domestic representative requirement — parallel to EU AI Act Article 22 (Authorised Representatives). Foreign-developed AI systems affecting Korean users require Korean domestic representation. Financial institutions without Korean presence but whose AI systems affect Korean customers must designate a domestic representative. This applies to cross-border fintech AI services and multinational banks' AI systems targeting Korean retail customers.

### Article 43 — Administrative Fines (과태료)

1. An administrative fine of up to **KRW 30,000,000** (approximately USD 22,000 / EUR 20,000) shall be imposed on any of the following:
   - (1) A person who violates Article 31(1) by failing to provide advance notification (AI disclosure obligation to users)
   - (2) A person who violates Article 36(1) by failing to designate a domestic representative
   - (3) A person who fails to comply with a suspension or corrective order under Article 40(3)

2. Administrative fines under paragraph 1 shall be imposed and collected by the Minister of Science and ICT as prescribed by Presidential Decree.

**Penalty comparison (G4):**

| Regulation | Tier 1 (Most Severe) | Tier 2 (High-Risk/High-Impact AI) |
|---|---|---|
| Korean AI Basic Act (Art.43) | KRW 30M (~EUR 20K) | KRW 30M (~EUR 20K) |
| EU AI Act (Art.99) | EUR 35M or 7% worldwide turnover | EUR 15M or 3% worldwide turnover |
| NIST AI RMF | None (voluntary framework) | None (voluntary framework) |

Korean penalties are substantially lower than EU penalties, making EU AI Act compliance the more demanding regulatory standard for internationally operating Korean financial institutions. However, Korean penalties are more broadly applicable to domestic AI business operators regardless of size.

### Article 42 — Criminal Penalties

A person who violates Article 7(9) by disclosing to others or using for purposes outside official duties any secrets learned in the course of official duties shall be punished by imprisonment of up to 3 years or a fine of up to KRW 30,000,000.

### Summary — High-Impact AI Obligations for Financial Institutions

Financial institutions deploying credit screening or other High-Impact AI under Article 2(4)(사) must fulfill the following under Korean AI Basic Act:

**Pre-deployment obligations:**
- Article 33: Screen AI system to confirm whether it qualifies as High-Impact AI (self-assessment mandatory)
- Article 30(3): Endeavor to obtain voluntary verification and certification before deployment
- Article 35: Endeavor to conduct fundamental rights impact assessment before deployment

**Ongoing operational obligations:**
- Article 31(1): Notify users in advance that the product/service is operated by High-Impact AI (penalty: KRW 30M for violation)
- Article 34(1)(1): Establish and operate risk management measures throughout AI lifecycle
- Article 34(1)(2): Establish explanation mechanism for AI output, main criteria, and training data overview
- Article 34(1)(3): Establish and operate user protection measures
- Article 34(1)(4): Implement human oversight and supervision of High-Impact AI
- Article 34(1)(5): Prepare and retain documentation of safety and trustworthiness measures
- Article 32(1): Identify, assess, and mitigate risks throughout AI lifecycle; establish safety incident monitoring system (applies to AI above Presidential Decree computation threshold)

**Extra-territorial obligations (if applicable):**
- Article 36: Designate domestic representative if operating from outside Korea
